About Me

I am a Computer Science graduate (First Class with Distinction) with strong foundations in data structures and algorithms, operating systems, computer networks, and artificial intelligence. I completed my B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Technological University (DBATU), where I ranked among the top students and was the First-Year batch topper with a CGPA of 9.89.

My interests center on applied engineering and research at the intersection of intelligent systems and scalable software architectures. As Technical Lead of BitsToBytes, the university’s CS technical group, I organized hackathons and peer-driven initiatives, developing early leadership and systems-thinking skills. My final-year project explored Web3-based blockchain architectures integrated with AI-driven mechanisms and resulted in a published patent. In parallel, I collaborated with my faculty advisor on research activities, contributing to literature review, experimentation, and thesis development.

I currently work at Johnson Controls on enterprise AI and cloud-native systems, contributing to distributed architectures and production-grade ML/LLM integrations for real-world customer experience platforms. I now aim to pursue graduate research in applied AI, distributed systems, and agentic architectures, focusing on building reliable, scalable, and deployable intelligent systems.
